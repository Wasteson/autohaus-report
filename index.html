Powertrain Update:
Germany, Oct 2025 – Mar 2026

How are different powertrain types developing in the German used-car market — and what does the new EV subsidy mean for young used BEV pricing?

Dealer listings · DE Young used ~12 months Baseline Oct/Nov 2025 = 100 v5 · 2026-04-05
Context

Why we focus on young used BEVs

Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs) are fully electric cars — powered entirely by a rechargeable battery, with no combustion engine. In Germany, a new private EV subsidy scheme came into effect on 1 January 2026, designed to stimulate sales of new electric vehicles.

When new BEVs become cheaper through subsidies, demand may shift away from young used BEVs — since the price gap between new and nearly-new shrinks. This analysis examines whether that tension is visible in listing data for young used cars (approximately 12 months old).

i

What we measure: This is a flow analysis — we track new listings created each month, not total inventory. These are asking prices, not transaction prices. A rising index reflects sellers pricing higher on new listings; it does not confirm what cars actually sell for.

Key findings

BEV pricing

BEV asking prices and residual value rise in tandem — a market under tension

The most significant pattern across the six-month period is the sustained upward movement in BEV asking prices — reaching approximately 110 in March 2026, up from 100 at baseline. Diesel and Petrol remain broadly stable, confirming this is a powertrain-specific dynamic rather than a general market shift.

For BEV, both asking price and RV%-index move in the same direction and at a similar magnitude. This is consistent with sellers' pricing ambitions broadly across models — not merely a change in which models are coming to market.

The residual value (RV%) index measures how cars are priced relative to their original new-car reference price. When both asking price and RV% rise together, it indicates that sellers are actively defending price levels relative to what the car cost new — not just a coincidence of different models entering the market.

This is consistent with a market under tension: sellers holding asking prices up on new listings, even as days-on-market increase — a pattern consistent with the analysis in Autohaus Magazin (February 2026), now confirmed and strengthened over a longer six-month period.

BEV segment detail

Within BEV

Different dynamics across body types

EstateCar (station wagons) shows the strongest absolute price increase — dominated by premium models with high reference prices, where absolute movements are large but the RV%-index movement is more moderate.

OffRoad and Limousine (SUVs and sedans) show stronger RV%-index increases. These mid-range segments are pricing more aggressively relative to their original new-car price — this is where the tension between supply and pricing ambition is most visible.

The divergence is analytically meaningful: EstateCar's high absolute prices partly reflect the premium models in that segment, while OffRoad and Limousine's RV% strength suggests broader price pressure relative to new-car reference prices.
