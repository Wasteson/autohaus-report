Methodology

What this analysis measures

This analysis tracks new used-car listings created by dealers in Germany — what we call the flow lens. Unlike a stock snapshot of all inventory, flow data captures the earliest signal: what sellers price cars at the moment they enter the market.

These are asking prices — we do not observe what cars actually sell for. All series are indexed with October/November 2025 = 100, and we focus on young used vehicles approximately 12 months old.

Key finding

BEV asking prices and residual value rise in tandem

The most significant pattern across the full six-month period is the sustained upward movement in BEV asking prices — reaching approximately 109 in March 2026, up from baseline. Diesel and Petrol remain broadly stable, reinforcing that this is a powertrain-specific dynamic.

For BEV, both asking price and RV%-index move in the same direction and at a similar magnitude. This is consistent with sellers' pricing ambitions broadly across models — not merely a change in which models are coming to market.

This pattern is consistent with the dynamics described in Martin Verrelli's February 2026 Autohaus Magazin analysis: a market under tension where sellers are holding prices up on new listings, even as days-on-market increase. The updated six-month picture confirms and strengthens that finding.

BEV segment detail

Different dynamics within BEV

Within BEV, the three largest segments show meaningfully different patterns. EstateCar shows the strongest absolute price increase in asking prices — dominated by premium models with high reference prices, where absolute movements are large but RV% movement is more moderate.

OffRoad and Limousine show stronger RV%-index increases. These mid-range segments are pricing more aggressively relative to original new-car price — this is where the tension between supply and pricing ambition is most visible in the data.

Method

How this analysis is produced

All results are descriptive patterns in listing data. Month-to-month movements may reflect market dynamics, mix shifts, seasonality, or data artefacts. No causal claims are made.
